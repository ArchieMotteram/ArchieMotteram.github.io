AI & the Australian Economy

By 2030, artificial intelligence will become one of the most important economic technologies in Australia since the internet. Its primary impact is task transformation, not full job replacement.

Australia’s productivity growth has been weak for years. AI offers a potential shift by reducing the cost of cognitive labour in a service-based economy.

However, benefits will be uneven: high-skill workers and large firms are likely to gain the most.

AI as a General-Purpose Technology

AI is a general-purpose technology (GPT) because it spreads across many sectors and requires complementary organisational change.

Like electricity and computing, its biggest productivity effects only appear when firms restructure workflows around it.

In Australia’s service economy, AI is especially powerful because it reduces the cost of information processing across law, finance, healthcare, and education.

Economic Channels

AI affects the economy through productivity gains, labour augmentation, innovation, and market concentration.

Industry Impacts
