AI and the Australian Economy

Productivity boom or inequality deepener? A forecast to 2030.

Thesis Economic Channels Industry Scenarios Policy

Thesis

By 2030, AI will likely raise productivity in Australia through task automation and augmentation rather than mass job replacement. However, without strong diffusion and policy support, the gains will be uneven and concentrated among high-skill workers and large firms.

Economic Channels

1. Productivity: Faster drafting, coding, analysis, and reporting.

2. Augmentation: AI improves worker output rather than replacing roles.

3. Innovation: Lower cost of experimentation for firms and startups.

4. Concentration: Large firms gain more due to data and systems.

Industry Impacts

Professional Services: High exposure, workflow redesign

Healthcare: Strong augmentation, admin reduction

Education: Personalised learning + admin support

Finance: Efficiency + risk modelling improvements

Government: Faster service delivery if adoption succeeds

Scenario Explorer

Productivity: Moderate

Employment: Stable

Inequality: Medium

AI adoption grows steadily but unevenly, with gains concentrated in large firms.

Policy Response

Skills: Lifelong learning and workforce transition programs

Diffusion: Support SMEs to adopt AI tools

Competition: Prevent market concentration

Government role: Public sector as early adopter
