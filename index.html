AI & the Australian Economy

A 2030 forecast of productivity growth, labour transformation, and inequality dynamics.

Core Insight

AI increases productivity through task automation and augmentation.

Key Risk

Benefits may concentrate among high-skill workers and large firms.

Policy Issue

Diffusion of AI across SMEs and public institutions determines outcomes.

Thesis

By 2030, AI will raise productivity in Australia primarily through task augmentation rather than mass job replacement. However, without strong diffusion, skills investment, and competition policy, gains will be uneven and concentrated.

Economic Channels

Productivity

Automation of drafting, coding, and administrative tasks increases output per worker.

Augmentation

AI complements skilled labour, increasing efficiency rather than replacing roles.

Innovation

Lower experimentation costs accelerate startups and product development.

Concentration

Large firms gain disproportionate advantage through data and infrastructure.
