AI & Australian Economy

By 2030, artificial intelligence will become one of the most important economic technologies in Australia. Its primary effect is not full automation, but task-level transformation of work.

Australia’s key constraint is weak productivity growth. AI may reverse this trend by lowering the cost of cognitive labour in a service-based economy.

However, the gains are likely to be uneven. High-skill workers and large firms benefit most due to complementary capital, data, and organisational capacity.
